A Day in the Life

1. PRE-SALES

Imagine a potential customer emails you with the following question. We don’t yet offer this feature, but their current solution does. How would you respond?  

“I’m considering switching to Product Customizer. Does your app allow live image previews?”

“Product Customizer currently does not support this feature, though you can easily …” (provide the simplest workaround to diminish lack of this feature)
“Product Customizer also allows you to …” (present advantages of Product Customizer over current solution based on research of potential customer and current solution)

IF I have permission to do this (leveraging exclusivity to entice customer to try Product Customizer):

“We normally only offer 2-week trials, but I’ve gone ahead and asked for special permission to set you up with a 1-month trial since I’m sure Product Customizer will …” (emphasize what pains and problems Product Customizer will annihilate for the client/why I'm sure they'll be hooked once they give it a try)
“Let me know if you’re interested, and feel free to let me know if you have any other questions.”

2. FIRE ALARM

Imagine you’ve just started your workday. You login and see a large number of emails from distraught customers. Clearly, something is happening. What steps do you take?

  1. Identify the common issue(s) and severity level(s)
  2. Report issue(s) to developers and other appropriate parties
  3. Send email to inform all affected customers of the current issue, fix status/ETA, and a temporary workaround if one exists
  4. Respond to each individual complaint as necessary for damage control

3. STEP-BY-STEP

We’d like to get an idea of how you explain concepts and processes. What was the last thing your purchased online? Tell us, step-by-step, how you went about searching, choosing, and completing your purchase.

I needed to purchase a replacement cable for my Sennheiser IE80 earphones.

  1. Searched “ie80 replacement cable” on Google
  2. Scanned top results to identify potential distributors and marketplaces to purchase from
  3. Determined eBay was the best option due to largest selection and lowest prices
  4. Searched “best ie80 replacement cable” on Google
  5. Scanned top results to for user suggestions and reviews
  6. Learned that cable quality is insignificant in determining sound quality
  7. Searched “ie80 replacement cable” on eBay
  8. Scanned results to identify cheap and durable-looking cables
  9. Purchased the cable with the fastest shipping time
