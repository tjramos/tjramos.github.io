Principles

1. Purpose

What do you want a website for?

Do you just want to validate your existence or do you want people to actually show up and pay for what you offer?

Every visit is an opportunity to convert a viewer into a customer. Capitalize on this to grow your customer base and maximize sales.

Every aspect of your website should be designed with purpose.

2. Presence

Your website is the foundation of your online presence. It's the first impression most people will get about your business, and you never get a second chance to make a first impression.

A website that's difficult to navigate, looks ugly, or has mediocre content will hurt your reputation.

Your values shine through your website. Strive to impress.

3. Experience

Navigating your site is an experience that determines how prospective customers will perceive you.

It's often the first impression, and you'll never get a second chance.

Your site should be easy to navigate, have the right information, and present it in a compelling way.

4. Awareness

Your viewers will consist of varying awareness levels regarding what you offer.

Some know who you are, what you offer, and are just waiting for the right deal.

Some know they want what you offer, but donâ€™t know you offer it.

Some have no idea that what you offer even exists, but are the ones that need it the most.

Aim with precision and don't miss any easy targets.

5. Action

To attract and convert customers, your website must compel viewers to take action.

The right action always relates to your sales funnel - subscribe to your email list, read the next article, or buy your product.
